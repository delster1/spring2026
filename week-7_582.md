 ### Summary of presentation (150-500 words)
 Valter talked about his experience at Microsoft and how to sell yourself in industry. He introduced the notion of a walk-in deck, which included an intro about him, his community, his resume in one page, his achievements from the most recent year, and his technical excellence, thought leadership experience (presentations), and orchestration - how he works well with people. This shows that you're prepared to sell youself and to talk about yourself. He also spoke about the importance of getting certifications, keeping track of experience, and the importance of continuous learning. He spoke about the importance of letting a comapny pay for education, this can set you up extremely well early in one's career.

### What did you learn from the presentation (50-150 words)?

One thing that I liked about Valter's presentation and learned was the notion of a walk-in deck. This is something that presents you personally and professionally, to sell yourself and to sell your brand personally. I was surprised by the importance Valter placed on the combination of critical thinking and generative AI. I was also surprised by the importance Valter placed on culture in his presentation. Being a member of a team who is a good "culture fit" is much more important to one's employment and success than I initally estimated.

### What will you do different because of this presentation (75-250 words)?

I'm going to make a walk-in deck and include it on my website. I'd love for my website to be a place to sell myself and to introduce my self to companies and people. I want to improve my website enough that I can be comfortable sending it to employers, and using it as a sort of walk-in deck. I'll also ensure I'm making myself a good culture fit at companies where I work by keeping a good relationship with my boss and my team members.
