 ### Summary of presentation (150-500 words)
Erin talked about her journey from being a computer science student with an interest in art and design into becoming a UX / UI engineer. She has a lifelong passion for art and has always wanted to find a way to combine art and CS. She describes user experience engineering (UX) as "logic you can see" - how we present, access, use, and understand the data we interact with. Some of the skills she mentions are relevant are her technical literacy, ability to estimate feasibility, and ability to translate between 'developer-speak' and human language. In her daily life, she spends time working with users to understand their interactions, understanding how to solve the problems that a user experience, and describing the problems, as well as a proposed solution, to developers.

### What did you learn from the presentation (50-150 words)?
I learned more about the importance of communication and collaboration in UX/UI engineering. The first critical thing a UX engineer must do is understand the pain-points of their users. This can only come about through communication and understanding. I also learned that UX/UI engineering is much more interesting than I initially estimated, I am a strong hater of javascript and web frameworks, but I really enjoy user-focused design and development. So I may need to give front-end more of a chance.


### What will you do different because of this presentation (75-250 words)?

I will keep UX/UI engineering in mind as I explore my career options. As a person who has strong skills in communication and collaboration, and finds the science of user interaction interesting, this domain of engineering is more fascinating to me than I initially estimated. I will do more to explore the current landscape of front-end design and understanding what UX/UI means outside of those experiences.
