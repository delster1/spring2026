### Name of Presentation
    - The Death of The Art of Computer Science
### Name of Presenter(s)
    - Sandy Urazayev

 ### Summary of presentation (150-500 words)
 He spoke about what AI is, how it's used in the real world, and the broader AI industry through his experience at an AI startup. He started with his perspective that AI is bad financially, as companies are spending huge amounts of money on data center development, AI spending, and big tech pushes. He also spoke about AI's impacts environmentally and personally. He spoke about AI chatbots deteriorating mental health to AI datacenter's negative impacts on their community, AI art and it's impacts on copyright, and AI's impact on the computer science industry, AI has been proven to not necessarily be a net gain to society. He moved on to how Microsoft uses AI - they use it as a primary tool every day for development and it accelerates his ability to develop and write quality code and to be a successful employee. He describes corporate BS as being stuck on a migration team and the little details that we all hate to do as developers. He concluded by confronting the contradiction that AI is killing us by stealing our art with his love of using and building with AI. This confrontation results in the understanding that software engineering is about creating business value, not creating and understanding art like we do in college. To stay ahead, we have to use the tools around us despite personal reservations, AI's rat-racey nature, and it's impact on our personal skills as developers.

### What did you learn from the presentation (50-150 words)?

I learned a lot about how AI is being used in industry. As someone in a company that is slightly behind the curve on AI due to industry regulations, seeing how a tech company leverages AI was incredibly interesting. AI accelerates his team's ability to save time. By delegating tasks that may be tedious, slow, or overbearing to tools that can create software, these tasks can be avoided almost entirely due to AI's capacity for automation. I've also always tried to think of companies and corporate structure as a really good codebase, Sandy brought an example that gives me a more fitting simile. A good corprate structure functions like a distributed system. 

### What will you do different because of this presentation (75-250 words)?

Get off of the migration team that I am currently on. I'm mostly joking, but I hope to apply AI to my current job in ways that I am not currently. Our team at my workplace is working on integrating AI into our current tooling system, but this is an effort I've been mostly hands-off on as I am only currently a part-time worker. When I go to full-time, I plan to deep-dive into our comapny's integration of AI and find ways to apply my knowledge to this sector.

 ### Summary of presentation (150-500 words)
 Valter talked about his experience at Microsoft and how to sell yourself in industry. He introduced the notion of a walk-in deck, which included an intro about him, his community, his resume in one page, his achievements from the most recent year, and his technical excellence, thought leadership experience (presentations), and orchestration - how he works well with people. This shows that you're prepared to sell youself and to talk about yourself. He also spoke about the importance of getting certifications, keeping track of experience, and the importance of continuous learning. He spoke about the importance of letting a comapny pay for education, this can set you up extremely well early in one's career.

### What did you learn from the presentation (50-150 words)?

One thing that I liked about Valter's presentation and learned was the notion of a walk-in deck. This is something that presents you personally and professionally, to sell yourself and to sell your brand personally. I was surprised by the importance Valter placed on the combination of critical thinking and generative AI. I was also surprised by the importance Valter placed on culture in his presentation. Being a member of a team who is a good "culture fit" is much more important to one's employment and success than I initally estimated.
